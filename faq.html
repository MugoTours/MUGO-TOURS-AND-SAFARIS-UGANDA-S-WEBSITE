RWENZORI MOUNTAINS CENTRAL CIRCUIT TREK

DAY 1: NYABITHABA CAMP 2650m asl: From Nyakalengija the starting point, you will hike though a thick tropical forest and climb up a step ridge with fields of ferns up to the the camp, this takes you 4-5 hrs hike.The camp is located in a dense forested canopy mixed up with podocupus trees. A view of bird species,black and white colobus monkeys, blue monkeys, butterflies and many more. Duikers and a mountain elephants can be seen by chance.

DAY 2: JOHN MATHE CAMP 3380m asl: From NYABITHABA to JOHN MATHE camp is a 6-7 hrs walk. The trail takes though the Kurt SChafer bridge at Mubuku/ Bujuku rivers conference, bamboo,heather with moss forest up to alpine zone of the giant lobelias and the groundsel to the camp surrounded by heather trees. The heather trees make its own zone however, you will find other species of trees like the St. John's wart, rapanea, giant lobelia, will be found in this zone. Animals like the rocky hyrax can be spotted or heard at night.

DAY 3: BUJUKU CAMP 3977m: From JOHN MATHE to BUJUKU camp will take you 4 to 5 hrs hike. The camp is located beneath/foot of the heart of Mt. Stanley, Mt. Baker, Mt. Speke and the camp is a place you will have a breath taking view of the glaciers and lake Bujuku just a close range. Bujuku is preferred as resting point for most peak climbers for acclimatisation. From here you can ably climb Mt. Speke and back the same day.

DAY 4: ELENA CAMP 4541 m: From Bujuku Camp to Elena Camp will take you 5 to 6 hrs hike. The Camp is established at the snow line as the starting point to the main high peaks of Margherita 5109 m and Alexandra both twin peaks on Mt Stanley. On this day, ensure that you have all the necessary snow ware equipments in order to ensure your safety. From Elena to the summit will take you 6 to 7 hrs hike.

DAY 5: KITANDARA LAKES CAMP 4027 m: From Elena camp to Kitandara lakes camp will take you 3 to 4 hrs hike. The camp is surrounded with senecio/ groundsels trees,lobelias,St. Johns wort. While at the camp you will be able to see the two beautiful upper and lower Kitandara lakes. Have a nice view of the Elena glaciers from this camp.

DAY 6: GUY YEOMAN CAMP 3450 m: From Kitandara to Guy Yeoman is 5 to 7 hrs descend after an early morning thigh bursting climb lead you to the fresh field pass at 4280 m to have a view of the Edward glaciers on Mt. Baker, Luigi du Savoie, Lakes of Kitandara and the eastern part of Democratic Republic of Congo. Guy Yeoman is located within a scenic valley of River Mubuku, Bujongolo, Kendahi rock shelters of the Duke of Abruzzi and Kabamba waterfalls are part of the amazing attractions you will enjoy as you descend to this camp. Guy Yeoman to Nyabithaba camp will take you 4 to 6 hrs descent and then Nyabithaba to Nyakalengijo/Ibanda is 3 to 4 hrs descent.

Note: MUGO TOURS AND SAFARIS UGANDA does not only climb the mountains but also will help you reach other places of interest in and outside Uganda.

Camps are all comfortable for your sleeping, however you need warm sleeping bags, warm cloths,climbing equipments and first aid kits.
Add extra days for more activities such as exploring Bakonzo traditional dance, meals, traditional healers, community interaction and many more while on trek and always seek advice from mugo tours and safaris uganda for the success of your trip.
